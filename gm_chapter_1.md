## Chapter Goal

By the end of Chapter 1 the PCs must have:
1. **Both halves of the treasure map** (one from Castle Rumplank, one from the Gasping Gipper).
2. **The sapphire scale** (from a thyalodon in the Writhing Swamp).
3. Assembled the map → it begins disintegrating → cliffhanger to Ch 2.

Party levels up to **2** at the end.

## Pacing at a glance

Order of play:

1. **Castle Rumplank** opening — meet royals, get map half.
2. **Run around Rumplank** — 3–5 of the 6 locations, in any order. Drop **Event 1 (Iron Fists)** after the first or second stop.
3. **Iron Eels' Hideout** — combat dungeon ending in the Melar Kan duel.
4. **Gasping Gipper** OR **Writhing Swamp** — either order.
5. **Map assembles** → cliffhanger.

> **If they get stuck** on where to go: drop **Event 3 — The Indigo Stranger**. She just hands them the lead to the Iron Eels' hideout.

## Scene 1 — Castle Rumplank (the hook)

- **Where:** Royal hall, [Castle Rumplank](gm_glossary.md#castle-rumplank).
- **Who:** [King Okeki](gm_glossary.md#king-okeki-redfeather), [Queen Maxelle](gm_glossary.md#queen-maxelle-redfeather), [Prince Kulupi](gm_glossary.md#prince-kulupi), [Princess Atriella](gm_glossary.md#princess-atriella).
- **Goal:** PCs accept the quest and receive **Map Half #1**.

### Read aloud (opening)

> *You find yourselves standing atop a wide swath of velvet carpet that cuts through the center of Castle Rumplank's magnificent royal hall. Nearby, the sweet smell of [Barnacle Bay](gm_glossary.md#barnacle-bay) wafts through the open glass windows. Before you stands a family of four beautiful [g'mayuns](gm_glossary.md#gmayun) in spectacular regal garb, flanked all around by countless royal guards. King Okeki — the family's dignified but slightly distracted-looking patriarch — eyes each of you carefully, then clears his throat.*

The king explains: a magical map fragment has hung in the royal hall for generations. It leads to **Poppy von Barnacle's hideout**. Find its missing half, follow it, claim the legacy. **All treasure is yours to keep.**

### Likely player questions (short answers)

- **Why us?** Honor for Rumplank; the prince hand-picked you.
- **How do the halves combine?** A keystone is needed — they don't know what it looks like, probably with the missing half.
- **Where to start?** Try the [**Temple of Many Colors**](gm_glossary.md#temple-of-many-colors), [**Quill & Scroll Academy**](gm_glossary.md#quill--scroll-academy), or wander [**Treasure Beach**](gm_glossary.md#treasure-beach). The queen mentions the [**Gutted Snake**](gm_glossary.md#gutted-snake) tavern in the [Claw Quarter](gm_glossary.md#claw-quarter).

### Recall Knowledge (secret roll, DC 15 Society or Rumplank Lore)

- **Crit success:** Poppy retired in secret; locals invent myths about her.
- **Success:** Rumplank is a port city founded 200 years ago by Poppy.
- **Failure:** Nothing.
- **Crit failure:** *Total nonsense* — Redfeathers are demon worshippers, Poppy is a false deity. (Just roleplay it as misinformation.)

### Hand over the map

The king presents a faded parchment torn along one edge. **It looks like a crude sketch of [Goldcrop Island](gm_glossary.md#goldcrop-island).**
- **HP 10**, fixable with *mending*.
- Protected by permanent 6th-level *nondetection*.

## Scene 2 — Running around Rumplank

The PCs visit any of these locations in any order. After 1–2 stops, drop **Event 1 (Iron Fists)** — see [Scene 3](#scene-3--three-street-events).

> Each location offers: a clue + a "bonus reward" if they engage with the NPC.
> **Standard XP: 10 each** for visiting, **30 each** if they earn the bonus reward.

---

### 2A. The Gutted Snake (worst bar in town)

**NPC: [D'marti Tixima](gm_glossary.md#dmarti-tixima)** (LN [g'mayun](gm_glossary.md#gmayun) ranger 1) — found the original map, then lost it.

**Goal:** PCs learn the [**Iron Eels**](gm_glossary.md#iron-eels) stole the other map half.

**Key facts D'marti shares**
- Found the map about a week ago in a cherry-wood scroll case in the jungle. Doesn't remember where (lost as usual).
- Showed it off here at the Gutted Snake. [**Squotan Malardy**](gm_glossary.md#squotan-malardy) (Iron Eel) stole it.
- Suspects the map is cursed. Suggests [**Jakapo Town**](gm_glossary.md#jakapo-town) (Poppy's wizard friend [Arahosko](gm_glossary.md#arahosko-the-red) was from there) for a cure.

**If players push the barkeep [Molo "Make-Sick" Dogodor](gm_glossary.md#molo-make-sick-dogodor):**
- DC 17 Intimidation → confirms Malardy stole it.
- Or DC 15 / 17 skill check on another patron for the same info.

**Bonus reward — pack D'marti's backpack**
- DC 15 Athletics (brute force) **OR** DC 17 Survival (organize neatly).
- Pick **2 of**: disguise kit, repair kit, climbing kit, firearm cleaning kit, healer's tools, snare kit.

**Companion:** D'marti can join as an NPC companion if they pack the bag and make a good impression.

**XP:** 10 each (talk) / **30 each** (helped pack).

---

### 2B. Nevergo's Antidotes (back-alley apothecary)

**NPC: [Captain Nevergo](gm_glossary.md#captain-nevergo)** — gravelly-voiced pirate-drawl shopkeeper. Also points to D'marti.

**Goal:** Confirms D'marti has a matching map. Sends them to the Gutted Snake.

> **GM tip:** This is a backup if PCs miss D'marti. Their map looks identical to the one D'marti showed Nevergo days ago.

**Bonus reward — sort his mixed-up flasks**
- He's mixed up wormwood and belladonna.
- DC 17 Crafting (Identify Alchemy) by tasting — **50% chance (DC 11 flat)** they taste belladonna instead → **DC 19 Fortitude save vs poison**.
- Safer: DC 20 Perception to remember which flask he grabbed from where.
- **Reward:** flask of belladonna.

**XP:** 10 each / **30 each**.

### 2C. Pekipak's Wagon (the [Twin Talons](gm_glossary.md#twin-talons))

**NPC: [Sagoya Pekipak](gm_glossary.md#pekipak-sagoya)** — g'mayun rat catcher, aspiring inventor.

**Goal:** Foreshadow the music-and-vermin trial of Chapter 3.

**The Rodential Respirator (R.R.)** — a vacuum tube. Pekipak wants someone to test it.
- Volunteer puts hand over the nozzle. **DC 15 Fortitude** or take **1d6+2 nonlethal bludgeoning**; on crit fail also **clumsy 1** for a day. On success the device just slips off the hand.
- Pays a gold coin regardless.

**What he tells them**
- Watch out for [**blood mice**](gm_glossary.md#blood-mouse) swarms in the jungle.
- Music can pacify big swarms — the bigger the swarm, the bigger the song. *(Foreshadowing: this matters at the Hissing Chasm in Ch 3.)*

**Bonus reward — improve the device**
- DC 15 Crafting **OR** DC 13 Engineering Lore.
- Crit success → he gives them a **fan feather token**.

**XP:** 10 each / **30 each**.

### 2D. [Temple of Many Colors](gm_glossary.md#temple-of-many-colors)

**NPC: [Prismatic Colvi](gm_glossary.md#prismatic-colvi)** — high priest. Helpful, ego-driven.

> **GM-only:** Colvi is a powerful cultist of the Shivering Behemoth. He gives no tells now. **Track how the PCs treat him** — he comes back as a major villain in Ch 12. Do not reveal anything.

**Goal:** PCs learn the map needs a **keystone** to be reassembled, and that the map will **disintegrate** once read.

What Colvi tells them (after examining the map):
- Two halves need a magical keystone (he doesn't know what it looks like).
- Once reunited, the map is warded with disintegrating magic — read it fast.

**Bonus reward — identify three lava-rock statues**
- Trained in Religion, **DC 18 Religion** to ID each (one attempt per character per statue).
- Statues are: a winged figure ([Pazuzu](gm_glossary.md#pazuzu) — Colvi tells acolytes to "take the cursed thing back" if PCs ID it), a g'mayun with a cane ([Sibling of the Past](gm_glossary.md#sibling-of-the-past)), an [orpok](gm_glossary.md#orpok) with a belly ([Ebrugeses](gm_glossary.md#ebrugeses)).
- ID at least 2 → reward: **4 common bludgeoning weapons** of choice + **one +1 weapon (2)** of choice.

**XP:** 10 each / **30 each**.

### 2E. Master Olo's Beach Fighting Camp

**NPC: [Master Olo](gm_glossary.md#master-olo)** — squat orpok martial arts master, on Treasure Beach (or any beach). Trains 3 [chochori](gm_glossary.md#chochori) students.

**Goal:** Olo saw an Iron Eel sprinter (Squotan Malardy) running through Claw Quarter clutching a similar map.

**Optional fight: Two Flying Pig Disciples** *(weak chochori wardens, Battlezoo Bestiary 9 / page 386, Creature 1)*. Nonlethal damage; they're fish out of water on land.

**Bonus reward — beat both with no PCs going down**
- Olo teaches the **Flying Pig Stance** and **Flying Pig Rush** feats (page 378).
- A PC may retrain a feat now to take Flying Pig Stance.

**XP:** 10 each / **30 each**.

### 2F. Quill & Scroll Academy

**NPC: [Headmistress U'oko](gm_glossary.md#headmistress-uoko)** — busy g'mayun scholar, fiddling with a puzzle cube.

**Goal:** She gives lore on Poppy's old crew. **The trivia she shares matters at G25 in Chapter 4** (Poppy's proving grounds final test).

**Poppy's companions she names** *(see [Fortunate Four](gm_glossary.md#fortunate-four))*
- [**Arahosko the Red**](gm_glossary.md#arahosko-the-red) — evoker; from Jakapo Town; weakness was the color blue.
- [**Jesea Swiftarrow**](gm_glossary.md#jesea-swiftarrow) — ranger with an X-shaped scar below her left ear.
- [**Pinomeri Elemarte**](gm_glossary.md#pinomeri-elemarte) — drifting cleric of Ebrugeses; loved any drink.
- [**Undayo of Stonegulch**](gm_glossary.md#undayo-of-stonegulch) ("Old Rock") — [kragrak](gm_glossary.md#kragrak) barbarian; only respected combat strength.

She also confirms the map's self-destructive magic (same as Colvi).

**Bonus reward — solve her petrified-wood puzzle cube**
- DC 15 Thievery (Disable a Device).
- Reward: 3 × 1st-level spell scrolls — **detect poison**, **jump**, **mending**.

**XP:** 10 each / **30 each**.

> **Trigger:** After the Academy visit, consider running **Event 2 — Glitterfest Parade**.

## Scene 3 — Three street events

### Event 1 — Iron Fists *(Low 1)*

**When:** After the PCs ask around 1–2 locations.

Two **Iron Eel goons** — Crackmaw and Litzi — confront the PCs in Claw Quarter, demanding info on the map.
- They flee through alleys at 5 HP or below, or after one drops.
- If captured, Crackmaw mocks them and dares them to come to the Iron Eels' hideout near [**Ironsalt Cove**](gm_glossary.md#ironsalt-cove).

**Stats:** Iron Eel Goon (Battlezoo Bestiary 34, Creature 0). Treasure: [monster parts](gm_glossary.md#monster-parts) (slashing/piercing weapons), 2.25/5/9 gp per goon.

> **Note:** This is the PCs' likely first fight. Remind them about monster parts if you're using that system.

---

### Event 2 — [Glitterfest](gm_glossary.md#glitterfest) Parade *(Trivial 1)*

**When:** After the Quill & Scroll visit (or whenever pacing needs a break).

Read aloud:

> *Bits of confetti and glitter waft on the breeze, sticking to your clothes and clouding your vision, before the merrymakers responsible for the mess appear from around a corner. Dozens of Rumplankers dance, sing, and laugh as they march down the street, oblivious to their surroundings. It's impossible not to get swept up in the revelers' good cheer — or their quickening pace.*

- **To stay still:** DC 17 Fortitude or Athletics.
- **To keep pace:** DC 17 Reflex or Performance.
- *(Either save can be replaced by DC 15 Party Lore.)*
- Fail → 1d4 nonlethal bludgeoning + glitter on clothes.
- Crit fail → glitter on face → **dazzled 1 minute** (or until cleaned with an Interact).

**Then:** A **[toogtoog](gm_glossary.md#toogtoog) spill** — caged toogtoogs spring loose, drawn to the glitter. They charge any glittered PC, otherwise an innocent reveler.
- **Toogtoog Spill** (page 403, Creature 1).
- Calm them peacefully (e.g., **Wild Empathy**) → toogtoog monger pays them the value they'd have got from kills.

---

### Event 3 — [The Indigo Stranger](gm_glossary.md#indigo-stranger) *(no combat)*

> **GM-only:** This is [**X'na'zed**](gm_glossary.md#xnazed), an [indigo dragon](gm_glossary.md#indigo-dragon), in g'mayun disguise. He uses the false name [**Zanaya**](gm_glossary.md#zanaya). He wants the cult-relic-jewel found because he loves Rumplank — he doesn't realize he's helping the cult.

**Use this only if PCs are stuck.**

A short g'mayun woman with **brilliant indigo eyes** in red robes approaches. Tells them:
- **Melar Kan** has the other half of the map.
- Hideout is in the **Claw Quarter**.

If asked her name → "Zanaya," "a helpful stranger." She'll feed them any clue you need to push the story forward.

> **Don't overuse her** — players notice railroading.

## Scene 4 — [Iron Eels'](gm_glossary.md#iron-eels) Hideout

A converted abandoned distillery near Ironsalt Cove, ringed by a moat. Three stories + a crow's nest.

**Hideout Features:** crumbling masonry exterior, wooden interior walls, 8-ft ceilings, all doors unlocked, dim light through cracks/windows.

| **A3** Mess | — | Red-herring map drawn from memory by a goon |
| **A4** Hall | — | Empty rum barrels (3 Bulk each) block A5's door |

### A5 — Clancy's magical gumbo

If unaware → he feigns being "just a chef" then hurls the pot.
- **15-ft cone, 1d4 fire, DC 15 basic Reflex.**
- Fail/crit fail → also exposed to magical gumbo (DC 15 Fort save vs. transmutation):
  - Fail → **clumsy 1**, fists become stone (lose nonlethal trait, +1 damage die), 1 hour.
  - Crit fail → 24 hours.
  - Volunteer-drinking automatically crit-fails the save.
- **Treasure on cutting board:** chunk of **calcite + yaramite** (1 Bulk, calcite worthless, yaramite worth **10 gp** to a collector).

### A11 → A14 — [Melar Kan](gm_glossary.md#melar-kan)

**Melar Kan** (Creature 1, unique LE g'mayun pirate captain)
- AC 16; HP 20; Fort +10, Ref +7, Will +4
- Speed 25 ft
- **+1 dagger** (+8 to hit, agile/finesse/magical/versatile S, 1d4+4 piercing)
- Spiked chain (+7, disarm/finesse/trip, 1d8+4 slashing)
- Fists +7 (agile/nonlethal, 1d4+4)
- Thrown dagger +8 (10 ft)
- **Kick 'Em While They're Down** (1/round, free): after a successful Trip, makes a fist Strike against the tripped creature.
- Surrenders at 10 HP if his crew is also down.

**The Pirate Code Duel offer:**
> *"Let's sort this out like sensible people, alright?"*

He proposes one PC vs. Melar Kan, weapons in a chest at the base of the crow's nest. PC wins → he reveals what he knows. Melar Kan wins → "you'll become our servants" + lose your map and gear.

If PCs refuse / hesitate → Melar Kan and remaining goons attack normally.

---

### Event 4 — Duel atop the Crow's Nest *(Moderate 1, 80 XP)*

**Setup**
- Both climb. Weapons in the chest below.
- No one else allowed on the nest.

**Hidden knife trick**
- A **+1 dagger (2)** is wedged into the underside of the crow's nest.
- Spotted from the rooftop on **DC 23 Perception**.
- A PC climbing the ladder can palm it on **DC 18 Thievery** (also requires DC 18 Perception to see it). If they don't, **Melar Kan palms it.**
- Without the dagger he only has fists.

**Greater cover from below** — attacks from below at the crow's nest are almost useless. But:
- The Iron Eel goons below try to **Demoralize** (Intim +5) the PC up top.
- **Smart players will realize:** the duel rules don't stop *other* PCs from attacking the goons below.

**Resolution rules:** standard combat, OR the duel subsystem from the *PF2 Gamemastery Guide* — Melar Kan is more dangerous in the duel.

> **Killing Melar Kan** — Don't auto-kill him at 0 HP. Knock him out so the PCs can extract info, or set him up as a recurring villain. If they kill him, his **journal in A11** has all the same info.

**Special:** A PC with the **indigo pirate background** who wins the duel earns rep — surviving Iron Eel goons offer to crew up if the PC ever gets a ship.

---

### A Deal's a Deal — what Melar Kan reveals

Whether through duel, fight, or journal:

1. **Other map half** is on the **Gasping Gipper**, a beached ship south of Goldcrop. *("It quite literally walked away.")*
2. **Sapphire scale** (the keystone) was stolen by **Squotan Malardy**, who fled into the **Writhing Swamp**. Probably sold it to a galtzagorri in Jakapo Town.
3. **Map is probably cursed** — the curse activates once it's reassembled. *"You'd have to find the map's maker — or someone like her — to undo it."*

**XP:** +30 story XP each.

---

### What if the PCs lose?

They wake up next morning in **A8 (sleeping room)**, bandaged, press-ganged into mopping the hideout. They:
- Overhear Melar Kan recapping the Gasping Gipper trip.
- Or rally the discontented Iron Eels.
- Last resort — Indigo Stranger or Prince Kulupi rescue them.

## Scene 5 — The [Gasping Gipper](gm_glossary.md#gasping-gipper)

- **Travel:** Rumplank → Gipper, **6 hours** (6 mi through difficult terrain).
- **Goal:** Recover **Map Half #2** from a **scroll mold** in the cargo deck.

### Ship features
- Rotten thin wood doors: Hardness 5, 20 HP, BT 10.
- Lower deck (B4–B7): 6-ft ceilings, dim light from hull cracks.
- Spaces below the waterline = **greater difficult terrain** (or Swim, DC 12 Athletics).

### B1 — Rocky Shoreline
No encounter. Lookout point — they can spot the skeletons on deck.

### B2 — Upper Deck *(Trivial 1)*
**2 Skeletal Iron Eels** (Creature −1) shamble around.
- AC 15; HP 5; negative healing; immune death/disease/mental/paralyzed/poison/unconscious; resist cold/electricity/fire/piercing/slashing 5.
- Talon +6 (agile, 1d4+2 slashing) + **bone ticks** disease (DC 14 Fort).
- A rusty grate in the center can be pulled up → drop 6 ft to **B6**.
- The captain's door (to **B3**) needs **DC 20 Athletics** to Force Open (table jammed against it).

**Hazard — Rotten Planks (×2)** *(Hazard 1)*
- Stealth DC 20.
- Disable: DC 15 Crafting or Thievery (trained).
- 2d6+5 slashing on trigger; **DC 17 basic Reflex**. Fail → flat-footed; crit fail → also prone.

### B3 — Captain's Quarters
- **Clue (in desk drawer):** the ship's manifest. A 200-year-old entry lists a **"fine wooden scroll case, cherry"** — Poppy's package, intended for [**Moonshadow Island**](gm_glossary.md#moonshadow-island), stored among other small items in the **cargo deck (B5)**.
- Hole in the floor → drops to **B7** (the [wispy wayfarer](gm_glossary.md#wispy-wayfarer) waits, disguised as a sheet).
- **Treasure** (if not using monster parts): lesser juggernaut mutagen, oil of weightlessness, full plate.

### B4 — Crew's Quarters
No encounter.
- **Treasure under pallets:** **+1 warhammer (2)**, 2 lesser bottled lightning, 2 light hammers.

### B5 — Cargo Deck *(Trivial 1)* — **the scroll case is here**

**Encounter: Scroll Mold** (Battlezoo Bestiary 86, Creature 0).
- Spot the bobbing scroll case: **DC 18 Perception** while Seeking.
- The mold is **not a real threat** — it casts *longstrider* and flees toward the prow, **trying to lead the party deeper into B6/B7**.
- It surrenders the second it takes any damage if cornered alone.

> **GM-only:** A fragment of Poppy's consciousness lives in the mold. If befriended, it conveys via hazy images: **mushrooms (Jakapo Town)** + **buzzing wings (galtzagorris)** = the Jakapo cure for the curse. Recognize: DC 14 Indigo Isles Geography Lore or DC 17 Society.

**Map retrieval:** Open the cherry scroll case (Interact). Map inside is dry. **If they already have the sapphire scale, they can try to assemble it now** — see [Scene 7](#scene-7--assembling-the-map).

**Optional companion:** if they treat it kindly, the scroll mold can travel with the party.

**XP:** 30 each for cornering the mold.

### B6 — Main Deck *(Moderate 1)*
**2 Springjaw Dolls** (Battlezoo Bestiary 108, Creature 1) — adorable sailor-suit dolls in standby. Eyes click open and they attack.
- Greater difficult terrain underwater (or Swim DC 12).

### B7 — Haunted Hold *(Moderate 1)*
**Wispy Wayfarer** (Battlezoo Bestiary 100, Creature 2) — the dead captain. Lies disguised as a sheet on the intact footlocker. **DC 22 Perception** to ID through the dim light.
- Climb up through the ceiling hole: DC 15 Athletics.
- **Hazard — Rotten Planks (×3)** (same as B2).
- **Treasure in footlocker:** **+1 rapier (2)**, 20 gp coins/gems. (If no monster parts: also +75 gp.)

## Scene 6 — The [Writhing Swamp](gm_glossary.md#writhing-swamp)

- **Travel:** Rumplank → Writhing Swamp, **4 hours** (4 mi through difficult terrain).
- **Goal:** Recover the [**sapphire scale**](gm_glossary.md#sapphire-scale) — currently stuck on a [thyalodon](gm_glossary.md#thyalodon)'s forehead at C6.

### Stopping at Jakapo Town first?

If PCs visit Jakapo Town to ask about the stolen scale:
- The herder [**Kuaranda**](gm_glossary.md#kuaranda) explains she bought it from a g'mayun stranger, didn't know it was magical, attached it as a head ornament to her favorite **thyalodon**, which then went missing.
- She gives detailed directions to the swamp → leads to **C1**.
- **Don't run Events 5–8 in Jakapo Town yet** — those are for Chapter 2.

### C1 — Snake Pit *(Moderate 1)*

**Creatures:** **1 [Leshy Husk](gm_glossary.md#leshy-husk)** lookout (BB 44, Creature 0). Doesn't notice PCs until within 30 ft.

**Hazard — Snake Pit** *(Complex Environmental Trap, Hazard 2)*
- Already triggered.
- Stealth +8 (or +0 if branches are disabled).
- Disable: DC 14 Survival or Thievery (branches).
- 20-ft pit, dozens of agitated nonvenomous snakes.
- AC 18; Fort +10, Ref +5.
- Branches: Hardness 3, HP 15.
- Snakes: HP 20; immune precision/swarm mind; resist physical 6 (except bludgeoning); weak area damage 3 / splash 3.
- **Pitfall trigger:** falling damage (~10 bludgeoning); Grab an Edge DC 20.
- **Routine (1 action):** each round, every creature in the pit takes **1d10+4 piercing**, DC 20 basic Reflex.

**In the pit: [Ignatius Barkley](gm_glossary.md#ignatius-barkley)** (LG male [wildfire leshy](gm_glossary.md#leshy) fighter 1) — the last uncorrupted Embersand. Disarmed but the snakes can't get through his thorny branches. Wants to smash the husks who corrupted his family.

- Rescue him → he can join as an **NPC companion**, if they have under 2 already.
- **Story XP:** 30 each for the rescue.

### C2 — Ruined Columns
No encounter.
- **Treasure:** Barkley's untainted backpack: trail rations, bedroll, lesser antidote, lesser alchemist's fire, 5 tindertwigs.

### C3 — Lookout Ledge *(Moderate 1)*
- **2 Leshy Husks** (BB 44, Creature 0).
- **2 Skeletal Iron Eels** (page 38, Creature −1).
- Husks face west, undead pirates face east. If either pair spots PCs they shriek and all 4 attack.

## Scene 7 — Assembling the Map

The moment the PCs have **both halves + the sapphire scale**, describe what happens *(book GM narration, paraphrased)*:

- Hold the two pieces together while waving the sapphire scale over the torn edges.
- The parchment glows with a golden light that crescendos into a **blinding flash**.
- In place of the two faded scraps, they now have a **seamless (and a bit cryptic) treasure map**.
- Six pinpoints of light dot the map like a miniature constellation, forming an obvious path from **Jakapo Town to the base of Shivering Mountain**.
- The sapphire scale now glows a faint golden color, softly pulsing in a slow rhythm.

**Then the curse fires** *(again, book GM narration, paraphrased)*:

- After only a moment, the map's corners start to wear.
- Before their eyes, the map is **disintegrating into ash**.
- It's clear they'll need to find a way to stop the map's deleterious magic — and quickly.

**Cliffhanger — natural stopping point.** Level up to 2.

(If they need more XP, give a **30 XP story award** for assembling the map, or run a quick social encounter.)

**Sapphire Scale (Item 3, rare divination magical, 50 gp, held in 1 hand)**
- Placed on a map → reveals hidden cartographic notes. Gives **+1 item bonus to Survival to Sense Direction** while you have a suitable map.
- **Activate (Interact, 1/min):** hold up to your eye → next Seek this turn gets **+1 item bonus** to detect invisible creatures; on crit success against an invisible creature's Stealth DC, it becomes observed (until it Sneaks).

**Hand them the Gilded Treasure Map handout** (book pages 46–47) if you want.

**Next chapter:** they race to **Jakapo Town** to undo the curse before the map crumbles fully.
