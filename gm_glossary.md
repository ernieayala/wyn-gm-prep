## A

### Aeternal dragon
**Type:** Ancestry / dragon. Dragons that walk among mortal communities. Long-lived, intelligent, and far more common in the Indigo Isles than elsewhere — wild dragons especially. About 2% of Rumplank's population is aeternal dragons.

### Amber dragon
**Type:** Dragon. The friendliest, chattiest dragons — natural rumormongers. Charismatic, nosy, manipulative spies who collect and spread gossip (true or not) for entertainment. They prefer dense, talkative cities. *Amber dragons are the source of many of the wild Poppy rumors floating around Rumplank.*

### Amon
**Type:** Deity. God of revelry, paired with [Castilli](#castilli) as the deities of "balance in daily life." Worshipped widely in Rumplank as the patron of leisure and parties. Appears as a guest of honor at Founder's Day in Ch 9. *See: [The Balance](#the-balance).*

### Aoz
**Type:** Deity. Twin of Life, paired with [Zoa](#zoa). Ever-important on the Isles since residents see the cycle of life and death in their daily fishing/farming. *See: [The Balance](#the-balance).*

### Arahosko the Red
**Type:** NPC (deceased). "Shellfire" — a galtzagorri evoker, [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle)'s wizard companion and chief cartographer. Cursed the treasure map and forged the [sapphire scale](#sapphire-scale). Was from [Jakapo Town](#jakapo-town); famous weakness was the color blue. One of the [Fortunate Four](#fortunate-four).

## B

### Bacoo
**Type:** Ancestry. Fey shapeshifters descended from amphibious mangrove fey. Came to the Material Plane long ago, found it harsh, stayed anyway. They're tricksters and party-throwers who don't trust quickly — but trust for life once earned. *Less common in Rumplank than g'mayun/orpok/chochori.*

### The Balance
**Type:** Religion. The dominant Indigo Isles faith. Worships two parent deities — **Tova the Father** (Law) and **Lilin the Mother** (Chaos) — plus several paired Sibling deities like [Aoz](#aoz)/[Zoa](#zoa) (life/death) and [Amon](#amon)/[Castilli](#castilli) (revelry/restraint). [Prismatic Colvi](#prismatic-colvi)'s temple worships under this faith.

### Bannog
**Type:** Ancestry. Rabbit-folk who originate from the realm of dreams. They consider their dreaming life as important as their waking one. They can technically nibble on souls but choose not to.

### Barnacle Bay
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. The natural harbor where [Rumplank](#rumplank) sits. Calm blue waters, coral, sandy beach. The bay's name predates [Poppy von Barnacle](#poppy-von-barnacle) — pure coincidence. The setting for Act 3's climax in Ch 12.

### Barrel Square
**Type:** Place — Rumplank district. Also called "New Rumplank." Market quad, food huts, party space. Home of [the Gilded Pirate statue](#poppy-von-barnacle) and Pinkwater Well. The lively, colorful tourist face of the city.

### Beakbluster Hills
**Type:** Place — Rumplank district. Wealthy upland district of multi-story manors and imitation [tarukis](#taruki). Home to Rumplank's old-money aristocrats. Named for the brisk winds off [Barnacle Bay](#barnacle-bay).

### Blood mouse
**Type:** Creature — common Goldcrop vermin. Nasty rodents that swarm in the jungle. Many g'mayuns eat them as part of regular diet ("find a mouse, eat a mouse"). Used by Rumplankers in illegal fighting rings. **One is Riupito's pet "Squints" in the Iron Eels' hideout.**

### Bluebell Island
**Type:** Place — Indigo Isles. Home of [**Seaview**](#seaview), the [orpok](#orpok)-built city that's the second-largest settlement in the Isles. Site of the prestigious **Academy of Tastes** culinary school. Setting of Ch 5. Famous for its bluebell flowers and harvest.

### Buoyant heart
**Type:** Concept. Rumplank's quintessential spirit of generosity and good cheer — being free of worry, giving your whole heart to others. Often invoked alongside ["the never-ending gala."](#the-never-ending-gala)

## C

### Captain Nevergo
**Type:** NPC. Surly g'mayun apothecary in [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter). Speaks in heavy pirate drawl despite never having sailed. Real name was Nellard — locals dubbed him "Captain Nevergo" mockingly and it stuck. Friend of [D'marti Tixima](#dmarti-tixima). Sells poisons out the back; gives PCs a clue about D'marti.

### Castilli
**Type:** Deity. Goddess of restraint, paired with [Amon](#amon). Often invoked as a warning to lazy children. Visits Founder's Day with Amon in Ch 9. *See: [The Balance](#the-balance).*

### Castle Rumplank
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. The Redfeather royal seat. Built by [Poppy von Barnacle](#poppy-von-barnacle), [Arahosko](#arahosko-the-red), and her crew after the Battle of Rosewater to defend Rumplank. Where Chapter 1 begins.

### Chakisa
**Type:** Deity. God of summer. Worshipped on the Isles as a year-round patron rather than a seasonal one (the climate barely shifts).

### Chochori
**Type:** Ancestry. Beings of living coral in roughly humanoid shape. Mostly sea-dwelling — only ~4% of Rumplank's population. Each chochori has one of seven **chori** (see below) — personality types/societal roles, **not** genders. Drop the analogy if a chochori is in earshot — they're tired of correcting outsiders.

### Chori (chochori chora)
**Type:** Concept. The seven "chori" of [chochori](#chochori) — personality/societal roles tied to elemental affinities. Examples in the book:
- **Hypisk** (water) — gentle, contemplative, considered wise
- **Farose** — courageous, quick-thinking
*Don't conflate chori with gender. Chochori find this very rude.*

### Chromatic Queen
**Type:** Item — ship. [Poppy von Barnacle](#poppy-von-barnacle)'s legendary flagship. The PCs gain ownership of it at the start of Ch 5 to sail Act 2.

### Claw Quarter
**Type:** Place — Rumplank district. The rough/dangerous district. Pirates, charlatans, blood-mouse fight rings, perpetually overcast weather. Also home to artisans and street performers. The [Iron Eels](#iron-eels) base out of here, near [Ironsalt Cove](#ironsalt-cove).

### Crackbeak Salt Desert
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. Inhospitable salt desert in the Okapi Hills. Setting of part of Ch 10's overland chase.

### Crooked Terrace
**Type:** Place — main street of [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter). Where most of the district's tenements, gathering halls, fortune tellers, and street performers live.

### Crustyard
**Type:** Place — Rumplank district. The industrial hub of Rumplank's shipping operations. Shipwrights, dockhands, warehouses; ships load/unload here. Distinct from [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter).

### Cult of the Shivering Behemoth
**Type:** Organization. Demonic cult of [Varagix the Glimmering](#varagix-the-glimmering). [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) wiped them out in 5822, but a sect rose again in secret. They worship a giant slain undead behemoth they want to reanimate. Includes [Prismatic Colvi](#prismatic-colvi) and (revealed in Ch 11) [Prince Kulupi](#prince-kulupi). The campaign's hidden true antagonists.

## E

### Ebrugeses
**Type:** Deity. Goddess of feasts and good food. Often invoked to bless festival meals. The orpok statue [Prismatic Colvi](#prismatic-colvi) shows the PCs in Ch 1 is a depiction of her.

### Eldamon
**Type:** Concept / creature type. Elemental monsters slightly out of phase with reality. They dwell in creatures, places, and things across the Indigo Isles (more than elsewhere). Eldamon trainers and elemental avatars are more common here than anywhere else.

### The Eld
**Type:** Religion. Ancient elementalist faith predating [The Balance](#the-balance). Centered on elemental power and respect for [eldamon](#eldamon) and [vitae](#vitae) (nature spirits). Modern [Summer Moon Festival](#summer-moon-festival) rites stem from it.

## F

### Fortunate Four
**Type:** Group. [Poppy von Barnacle](#poppy-von-barnacle)'s legendary adventuring companions. Four of the named ones:
- [Arahosko the Red](#arahosko-the-red) — galtzagorri evoker, mapmaker
- [Jesea Swiftarrow](#jesea-swiftarrow) — hardriggan ranger
- [Pinomeri Elemarte](#pinomeri-elemarte) — indigo dragon cleric of Ebrugeses
- [Undayo of Stonegulch](#undayo-of-stonegulch) — kragrak barbarian

The trivia [Headmistress U'oko](#headmistress-uoko) shares about them in Ch 1 matters at G25 in Ch 4.

### Founder's Day
**Type:** Festival. Annual celebration of [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle)'s "founding" of Rumplank. Includes the Rum Run drunken obstacle course and the year's biggest parade outside [Nevala](#nevala). The cult attacks during Founder's Day in Ch 9.

## G

### Galtzagorri
**Type:** Ancestry. Tiny anxious sprites with **ladybug wings**. Industrious — they love to build. Carry hook spears. About 5% of Rumplank's population. [Jakapo Town](#jakapo-town) is a notable galtzagorri settlement of giant mushrooms.

### Gasping Gipper
**Type:** Place — shipwreck. A mercantile caravel that sank ~200 years ago south of Goldcrop Island carrying a cargo case for [Moonshadow Island](#moonshadow-island). Now haunted by skeletal Iron Eels and a wispy wayfarer (the dead captain). The other map half is here in Ch 1.

### Gilded Pirate
**Type:** Title. [Poppy von Barnacle](#poppy-von-barnacle)'s legendary persona. Statue of her stands in [Barrel Square](#barrel-square).

### Glitterfest
**Type:** Festival. Yearly Rumplank holiday celebrating glitter — the material, the concept, anything sparkly. Featured in Ch 1's Event 2 (the parade + toogtoog spill).

### G'mayun
**Type:** Ancestry. **Avian humanoids** resembling bipedal tropical parrots — beak, talons, feathers, halfling-height. Eyes shift colors with the [great song](#the-great-song). Hatch from eggs. ~67% of Rumplank's population (the dominant ancestry). Native to the Isles. *King Okeki, D'marti, Melar Kan, and most of Rumplank are g'mayun.*

### Goldcrop Island
**Type:** Place. The largest island of the Indigo Isles and home to [Rumplank](#rumplank). Mostly settled around the southern coast. Features tropical jungle, beaches, the [Writhing Swamp](#writhing-swamp), and [Shivering Mountain](#shivering-mountain).

### The Great Song
**Type:** Concept. A mystical resonance that connects all [g'mayun](#gmayun). Each g'mayun perceives it differently — as a feeling, an instinct, or sometimes a literal song. It's why their eyes shift colors. Long ago, demons tried to corrupt the Great Song; the [Iris Refrain](#iris-refrain) saved it. *G'mayuns get prickly if outsiders treat the song as quaint folklore.*

### Gutted Snake
**Type:** Place — Rumplank tavern. The worst bar in [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter). Run by [Molo "Make-Sick" Dogodor](#molo-make-sick-dogodor). Where [D'marti](#dmarti-tixima) accidentally showed off the treasure map and lost it to [Squotan Malardy](#squotan-malardy).

## H

### Hardriggan
**Type:** Ancestry. Mighty humanoids with **ram-like horns** and goat features. Densely muscular; live by personal power. Their social structures are called **Columns** — strict hierarchies where the strong protect the weaker in exchange for tribute. ~4% of Rumplank's population. *[Jesea Swiftarrow](#jesea-swiftarrow), Poppy's ranger companion, was a hardriggan.*

### Headmistress U'oko
**Type:** NPC. Old g'mayun scholar, head of [Quill & Scroll Academy](#quill--scroll-academy). In Ch 1 she shares lore on [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle)'s [Fortunate Four](#fortunate-four) — info that matters in Ch 4. Has a petrified-wood puzzle cube she can't solve.

## I

### Ignatius Barkley
**Type:** NPC. LG male wildfire leshy fighter, the last uncorrupted **Embersand** clan member. Found stuck in the snake pit at C1 of the [Writhing Swamp](#writhing-swamp). His clan was corrupted into [leshy husks](#leshy-husk) by a dryad husk named **Pyksara**. Possible Ch 1 NPC companion.

### Indigo dragon
**Type:** Dragon. Social, party-loving dragons. Charismatic, kind, exuberant. They take revelry seriously — they want parties to last and stay safe/inclusive. Bombastic emotions. *[The Indigo Stranger / X'na'zed](#xnazed) is one. So was [Pinomeri Elemarte](#pinomeri-elemarte).*

### Indigo Isles
**Type:** Place. The setting — an archipelago far from any continent. Most common ancestries (in order): [g'mayun](#gmayun), [orpok](#orpok), [hardriggan](#hardriggan), [kragrak](#kragrak), [chochori](#chochori). Stable tropical climate; rainfall is the only real seasonal variation.

### Indigo Stranger
**Type:** NPC encounter. The role [X'na'zed](#xnazed) plays whenever he appears to push the story along — disguised as a kind stranger with brilliant indigo eyes. In his g'mayun-woman form he gives the alias "[Zanaya](#zanaya)." Use sparingly so PCs don't feel railroaded.

### Iris Refrain
**Type:** Historical event. Centuries ago, demons tried to corrupt [the Great Song](#the-great-song) and turn g'mayuns into a cruel servant ancestry. The Iris Refrain was the moment g'mayuns broke free, though the great song was never quite the same after. *G'mayun cultural pain point — handle with gravity.*

### Iron Eels
**Type:** Organization. Pirate gang based in the abandoned distillery in [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter). Led by [Melar Kan](#melar-kan). Sail the white sloop [**Sharpened Bone**](#sharpened-bone). They stole the other half of the map. Ch 1 mini-boss faction.

### Ironsalt Cove
**Type:** Place — sub-zone of [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter). Western waterfront where the [Iron Eels' hideout](#iron-eels) sits.

## J

### Jakapo Town
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. Galtzagorri village built into a thicket of giant mushrooms. About 4 miles from [Rumplank](#rumplank). Home of [Arahosko](#arahosko-the-red); the only people who can lift the curse on Poppy's map. Setting of much of Ch 2.

### Jesea Swiftarrow
**Type:** NPC (deceased). Hardriggan ranger, "the Rugged Ranger." [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle)'s archery companion. Built the first hanging bridge from [Beakbluster Hills](#beakbluster-hills) to [Twin Talons](#twin-talons). Famous **X-shaped scar below her left ear** — origin unknown. One of the [Fortunate Four](#fortunate-four).

### Jewel of the Indigo Isles
**Type:** Item. The unholy relic of the [Cult of the Shivering Behemoth](#cult-of-the-shivering-behemoth). [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) split it into 4 pieces and hid them across the Isles after defeating the cult. The PCs reassemble it across Acts 1–2; the cult steals it back in Ch 9. **The "treasure" the PCs are unknowingly assembling is a cult relic.**

## K

### King Okeki Redfeather
**Type:** NPC. The current monarch of Rumplank — affable, dignified, a little distracted, slightly under his queen's thumb. Quest-giver in Ch 1. Great-great-grandson of Lady Kili (the first Rumplank monarch).

### Kragrak
**Type:** Ancestry. **Earthen folk** whose bodies look like rocks, sand, or volcanoes. Symmetrical as children — asymmetrical features (rocky shoulders, mismatched fingers) are a sign of adulthood, gained through two coming-of-age rituals (~12 and ~24). Voracious appetites. Connected to a secondary element. ~2% of Rumplank, but ancient on the Isles. *[Undayo](#undayo-of-stonegulch), Poppy's barbarian, was kragrak.*

### Kuaranda
**Type:** NPC. Galtzagorri herder in [Jakapo Town](#jakapo-town). Bought the [sapphire scale](#sapphire-scale) from a stranger and tied it to her favorite [thyalodon](#thyalodon) as a head ornament — that thyalodon is now in Ch 1 area C6 of the Writhing Swamp. Possible Ch 2 NPC companion.

## L

### Leshy
**Type:** Ancestry. Intelligent plant-folk formed by druids embodying [vitae](#vitae) (nature spirits) into plant bodies. Common across the Isles. **Wildfire leshies** are the local heritage — leshies whose forms represent the rejuvenating power of wildfires. *Allegedly proliferated by an ancient kragrak druid circle.*

### Leshy husk
**Type:** Creature. Twisted, corrupted leshies — formerly ordinary leshies who've been blighted into mindless servants. The [Embersand clan](#ignatius-barkley) in the [Writhing Swamp](#writhing-swamp) was turned into husks by a dryad husk named Pyksara.

## M

### Master Olo
**Type:** NPC. Squat orpok martial-arts master. Conducts his beach fighting camp on Treasure Beach (and others). Saw [Squotan Malardy](#squotan-malardy) sprinting through Claw Quarter. Can teach the **Flying Pig Stance** feat to PCs who beat his disciples.

### Melar Kan
**Type:** NPC. Leader of the [Iron Eels](#iron-eels). Cruel, calculating, sporting g'mayun pirate captain. His "weapon of choice" is the Iron Eels namesake — a chain of iron-cast giant eel bones. Ch 1 mini-boss; offers the duel atop the crow's nest.

### Molo "Make-Sick" Dogodor
**Type:** NPC. Surly g'mayun barkeep of [the Gutted Snake](#gutted-snake). Knows more than he lets on; will admit to seeing [Squotan Malardy](#squotan-malardy) steal the map fragment with enough Intimidation pressure. Spreads slanderous rumors about his rival Clamardinia Wakamarsis (owner of the Rumpled Feather).

### Monster Parts
**Type:** System. The campaign is built on the **Monster Parts** subsystem from the Battlezoo Bestiary. PCs harvest parts from defeated creatures to refine and imbue magic items. This replaces normal magic-item economy. *If your group isn't using it, the book provides replacement treasures (e.g., the Gasping Gipper's "if not using monster parts: +75 gp" notes).*

### Moonshadow Island
**Type:** Place — Indigo Isles. Home to outcasts and criminals. Originally where Poppy hid one jewel piece. Currently overrun by **Shadow Plane invaders** doing fleshweaving experiments. Setting of Chs 6–7.

## N

### Nevala
**Type:** Festival. The biggest Rumplank festival — a week-long celebration twice per year, descended from the original "Never-ending Gala" festival of Poppy's crew's descendants. Brings thousands of party pilgrims to the city.

### The Never-ending Gala
**Type:** Concept. Both the original name of [Nevala](#nevala) and a slang phrase for the Rumplank lifestyle in general — the perpetual party of life on Goldcrop Island.

## O

### Obsidian Star
**Type:** Item. The first piece of the [Jewel of the Indigo Isles](#jewel-of-the-indigo-isles), recovered from [Poppy's](#poppy-von-barnacle) hidden proving grounds at the end of Ch 4. [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) brought this piece personally to [Goldcrop Island](#goldcrop-island).

### Old Rumplank
**Type:** Place — Rumplank district. The oldest district. Pre-[Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) g'mayun and orpok traditions live here, untouched by the cosmopolitan rest of the city. Shrines, artisans, gift-giving micro-economy.

### Orpok
**Type:** Ancestry. Humanoids with **distinctly pig-like features** — porcine nose, floppy ears, tusks, cloven hooves. Heavy-built. Excellent senses of taste and smell. Large families (litters of 6–8); short lifespans (~35 years). Newcomers to the Isles; built [Seaview](#seaview) on Bluebell Island. ~10% of Rumplank's population. *[Master Olo](#master-olo) and Clancy the Iron Eels' chef are orpok.*

## P

### Pazuzu
**Type:** Demon lord. The winged statue [Prismatic Colvi](#prismatic-colvi) shows the PCs in Ch 1 depicts Pazuzu — Colvi tells his acolytes to take the "cursed thing" back downstairs if the PCs correctly identify it.

### Peerless Ruby
**Type:** NPC. A vermilion dragon and founder of a hidden galtzagorri jungle village. Allies with the PCs in Chs 10–11. Flies the PCs back to Rumplank when the cult chase escalates.

### Pekipak (Sagoya)
**Type:** NPC. Rough-and-tumble g'mayun rat catcher and aspiring inventor in [Twin Talons](#twin-talons). Builder of the **Rodential Respirator**. Foreshadows the Ch 3 music-and-vermin trial when he tells the PCs music can pacify big swarms.

### Pinomeri Elemarte
**Type:** NPC (deceased). [Poppy's](#poppy-von-barnacle) [indigo dragon](#indigo-dragon) cleric of [Ebrugeses](#ebrugeses), "the Drifting Cleric." Could rarely refuse a drink. One of the [Fortunate Four](#fortunate-four).

### Poppy Nights
**Type:** Festival. Springtime gathering in Blazing Meadow celebrating poppies and ushering in the dry season. Children dress up as the [Gilded Pirate](#gilded-pirate).

### Poppy von Barnacle
**Type:** NPC (deceased). The legendary "[Gilded Pirate](#gilded-pirate)" — actually a war hero who defeated the [Cult of the Shivering Behemoth](#cult-of-the-shivering-behemoth) in 5822, then split the [Jewel of the Indigo Isles](#jewel-of-the-indigo-isles) into 4 pieces and hid them. Founded modern Rumplank ~200 years ago, established her secret proving grounds, and went missing/presumed dead about 30 years before the campaign starts (5992). *The whole campaign hangs on her legacy.*

### Prince Kulupi
**Type:** NPC. Serious, competent g'mayun prince. Studious, less popular than his sister. Hand-picked the PCs for the quest. **Cult mastermind — revealed in Ch 11.** *Don't tip the GM-only twist.*

### Princess Atriella
**Type:** NPC. Daring, courageous g'mayun princess. Note-taker at the Ch 1 royal hall meeting. Tendency to rush into danger. Kidnapped by the cult in Ch 9.

### Prismatic Colvi
**Type:** NPC. High priest of [the Temple of Many Colors](#temple-of-many-colors). Ego-driven but charming. **Secretly a powerful cultist of the [Cult of the Shivering Behemoth](#cult-of-the-shivering-behemoth) — recurring villain in Ch 12.** Track how PCs treat him in Ch 1.

### Quill & Scroll Academy

## R

### Razorback Isle
**Type:** Place — Indigo Isles. An island that doesn't exist on present-day maps. Leads the PCs to [Runaway Reef](#runaway-reef) instead, where the final jewel piece is. Setting of Ch 8.

### Redfeathers
**Type:** Family. Rumplank's royal family. Members:
- [King Okeki](#king-okeki-redfeather)
- [Queen Maxelle](#queen-maxelle-redfeather)
- [Prince Kulupi](#prince-kulupi)
- [Princess Atriella](#princess-atriella)

Lady Kili (the first Rumplank monarch, instituted as ruler during the [Rumplank Renaissance](#rumplank-renaissance)) was the great-great-grandmother of Okeki.

### Restoration Day
**Type:** Festival. The Rumplank "new year" — the only holiday where imbibement is *discouraged*. Rumplankers do a thorough cleaning of homes, streets, and livers. Charity and neighborly reconciliation are emphasized.

### Rosewater
**Type:** Place — Rumplank district. The capital district — home of Castle Rumplank, Rosewater Keep, and bureaucracy. Named for the **Battle of Rosewater**, a pivotal conflict during the [Rumplank Renaissance](#rumplank-renaissance) when [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) led the defense against featherless invaders.

### Rum Row
**Type:** Place — sub-zone of [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter). Where Rumplank's distillers sell straight to buyers.

### Rumplank
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. The setting. Population ~12,100, mostly g'mayun. Constitutional monarchy under the [Redfeathers](#redfeathers). Known as the "City of Color" / "City of Colors." Famous for its endless festivals, its pirate history, and the legacy of [Poppy von Barnacle](#poppy-von-barnacle).

### Rumplank Renaissance
**Type:** Historical period. The era of explosive growth after [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) settled in Rumplank — opened trade, established the constitution and monarchy with Lady Kili, built [Castle Rumplank](#castle-rumplank). Rumplank went from sleepy hamlet to dominant Indigo Isles power.

### Runaway Reef
**Type:** Place — Indigo Isles. A reef hiding a sunken Cult of the Shivering Behemoth temple — where Poppy slew the high priest and the entire temple was cast into the sea. The final jewel piece is here in Ch 8.

## S

### Sapphire Scale
**Type:** Item. The magical lens [Arahosko](#arahosko-the-red) crafted from indigo dragon hide. The keystone needed to fuse the two map halves. **Item 3, rare divination magical, 50 gp** — gives +1 item bonus to Survival to Sense Direction with a suitable map; 1/min activate for +1 to detect invisible creatures. Squotan Malardy stole and sold it in Ch 1's backstory; Kuaranda put it on a thyalodon's forehead. Recovered in C6.

### Seaview
**Type:** Place — Bluebell Island. The orpok-built capital city of [Bluebell Island](#bluebell-island). Second-largest settlement in the Indigo Isles. Home of the Academy of Tastes culinary school. Setting of Ch 5.

### Sharpened Bone
**Type:** Item — ship. The white sloop of the [Iron Eels](#iron-eels), anchored in [Claw Quarter](#claw-quarter).

### Shivering Mountain
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. The tallest peak; named in honor of the demon lord [Varagix](#varagix-the-glimmering). The cult built their original base in caves beneath it (later called [Shiverskull Cave](#shiverskull-cave)). [Poppy's](#poppy-von-barnacle) hidden proving grounds are also under the mountain, built into the cult's old halls.

### Shiverskull Cave
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. The cave at the base of [Shivering Mountain](#shivering-mountain). Setting of Ch 3 — entrance to Poppy's proving grounds.

### Sibling of the Past
**Type:** Deity. The g'mayun statue [Prismatic Colvi](#prismatic-colvi) shows the PCs in Ch 1 (a g'mayun-shaped statue with a walking cane) is a depiction of this deity. Part of [The Balance](#the-balance).

### Squotan Malardy
**Type:** NPC (offscreen). [Melar Kan's](#melar-kan) traitor first mate. Stole [D'marti's](#dmarti-tixima) map fragment for the Iron Eels, then betrayed Melar Kan by also stealing the [sapphire scale](#sapphire-scale) and selling it to [Kuaranda](#kuaranda) in Jakapo Town. Hasn't been seen in Rumplank since. **Note: In Ch 1's C6 area, the leshy husks are sacrificing the very thyalodon Kuaranda put the scale on.**

### Summer Moon Festival
**Type:** Festival. Lunar observance with roots in old g'mayun druidic beliefs of [The Eld](#the-eld). Modern rites are pastiche — the originals are lost to time. Believed by druids and occultists to please the land and empower [eldamon](#eldamon) and [vitae](#vitae).

## T

### Taruki
**Type:** Architectural term. Ancient stone buildings made of lava rock, dating back to pre-[Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) Rumplank. A few still stand in [Old Rumplank](#old-rumplank); fancy imitations dot [Beakbluster Hills](#beakbluster-hills).

### Temple of Many Colors
**Type:** Place — Rumplank. Large stone temple west of [Barrel Square](#barrel-square). Worships [The Balance](#the-balance) and older faiths. Headed by [Prismatic Colvi](#prismatic-colvi). Visited in Ch 1.

### Thyalodon
**Type:** Creature. A native Goldcrop Island creature (Battlezoo, page 402, Creature 3). Ridable; can be domesticated; some Rumplankers eat them. **In Ch 1, [Kuaranda](#kuaranda)'s missing pet is one — the leshy husks are about to sacrifice it at C6.**

### Toogtoog
**Type:** Creature. Cunning, chubby, tiny **frog-like creatures** native to the Indigo Isles. Popular as pets — they have inkwells on their heads producing usable ink daily. In big swarms ("spills") they're a pest who devour ingredients. Featured in Ch 1's Glitterfest event.

### Treasure Beach
**Type:** Place — Goldcrop Island. The original sandy shoreline where [Poppy](#poppy-von-barnacle) first landed. [Master Olo](#master-olo) often holds his fighting camp here.

### Twin Talons
**Type:** Place — Rumplank district. Two massive, flat-topped rock pinnacles that look like hooked claws. Home to eccentrics, alchemists, engineers, and quirky criminals. Hanging rope bridges connect the pillars to [Beakbluster Hills](#beakbluster-hills). [Pekipak's](#pekipak-sagoya) wagon is here.

## V

### Varagix the Glimmering
**Type:** Demon lord. Patron of the [Cult of the Shivering Behemoth](#cult-of-the-shivering-behemoth). Wants to enslave the Indigo Isles. The shale behemoth Poppy slew was created in his service. Has a fondness for gems.

### Vitae

## X

### Xotlxotl
**Type:** Ancestry. Salamander-folk. **Cultural twist:** in their culture, harshness = caring (they're pushing you to grow), and helpfulness = condescension (they want you to stay weak). If a xotlxotl is friendly, they probably don't respect you.

### X'na'zed
**Type:** NPC. An [indigo dragon](#indigo-dragon) who loves Rumplank and wants the PCs' quest to succeed for the city's sake. Doesn't realize his help benefits the cult. Disguises himself with brilliant indigo eyes. Uses the alias "**Zanaya**" (or "a helpful stranger"). Story-helper if PCs get stuck. *See: [Indigo Stranger](#indigo-stranger).*
